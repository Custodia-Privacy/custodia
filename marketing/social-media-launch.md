### Post 2: Thought Leadership

**The real cost of non-compliance isn't fines. It's the things you don't see.**

Everyone focuses on the headline GDPR fine numbers — €20M, 4% of revenue. Those are the maximums. Most small businesses won't face anything close to that.

But the actual costs of non-compliance are more subtle and more common:

**Lost deals.** Enterprise buyers now routinely require privacy compliance certifications from vendors. "We have a cookie banner" doesn't cut it when the procurement team sends a 40-question privacy questionnaire.

**Ad spend waste.** Without proper consent management and Google Consent Mode v2, your EU ad campaigns run blind. No remarketing. Degraded attribution. You're paying for traffic you can't track or retarget.

**DSAR scrambles.** When someone emails "send me all my data" and you have 30 days to respond, scrambling through Stripe, Mailchimp, your CRM, Google Analytics, and your support desk costs real time and money.

**Insurance and liability.** Cyber liability insurance applications increasingly ask about privacy compliance posture. Weak answers mean higher premiums or denied coverage.

**Customer trust erosion.** Visitors who see a poorly implemented consent experience — dark patterns, no reject button, pre-checked boxes — lose trust before they even see your product.

The businesses that treat privacy as a feature, not a checkbox, will have an advantage.

That's why we built Custodia: https://privacy.pagebolt.dev
